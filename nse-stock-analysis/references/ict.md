# ICT (Inner Circle Trader) Concepts Reference

## Overview

ICT methodology is a price action-based trading framework that focuses on institutional order flow, liquidity, and market structure. This reference covers how to apply ICT concepts to NSE/Indian market trading.

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. Market Structure

Market structure refers to the pattern of swing highs and swing lows.

**Bullish Structure:** Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL)
**Bearish Structure:** Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL)

**Market Structure Shift (MSS):** When the prevailing structure breaks - e.g., a bullish market makes a Lower Low, signaling potential trend reversal.

**Break of Structure (BOS):** Price breaks a significant swing high/low confirming trend continuation.

### 2. Order Blocks (OB)

An Order Block is the last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move. Institutions place large orders here.

**Bullish Order Block:** The last bearish (red) candle before a strong bullish move up. Acts as future support.

**Bearish Order Block:** The last bullish (green) candle before a strong bearish move down. Acts as future resistance.

**How to trade:**
1. Identify a strong impulsive move
2. Mark the last opposing candle before the move
3. Wait for price to return to the OB zone
4. Look for confirmation (rejection wick, engulfing pattern)
5. Enter with stop-loss below/above the OB

### 3. Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

A Fair Value Gap occurs when there's an imbalance between buyers and sellers, creating a gap in the price.

**Bullish FVG:** Candle 1 High < Candle 3 Low (gap between them that price didn't fill)
**Bearish FVG:** Candle 1 Low > Candle 3 High

**How to trade:**
- Price tends to return to fill FVGs
- Use unfilled FVGs as entry zones
- Combine with order blocks for confluence

### 4. Liquidity

**Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL):** Stop-losses of short sellers above swing highs. Smart money targets these.

**Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL):** Stop-losses of long traders below swing lows. Smart money targets these.

**Liquidity Sweep:** When price briefly pierces a swing high/low to trigger stops, then reverses. This is a key entry signal.

### 5. Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

The OTE zone is the 62%-79% Fibonacci retracement of the most recent impulsive move. This is the ideal entry zone.

**How to calculate:**
1. Identify a clear impulsive move (impulse leg)
2. Draw Fibonacci from swing low to swing high (bullish) or high to low (bearish)
3. The 0.618-0.79 zone is the OTE
4. Wait for price to retrace into this zone
5. Enter with stop below the swing low (bullish)

### 6. Kill Zones (Mapped to IST)

ICT Kill Zones are specific time windows when institutional activity peaks.

| Kill Zone | Original (ET) | IST Equivalent | Relevance to NSE |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Asian Session | 8:00 PM - 12:00 AM ET | 5:30 AM - 10:30 AM IST | **NSE Market Open** - highest relevance |
| London Open | 2:00 AM - 5:00 AM ET | 12:30 PM - 3:30 PM IST | **NSE Afternoon** - moderate relevance |
| NY Open | 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM ET | 6:30 PM - 9:30 PM IST | Post-market for NSE |

**For NSE:**
- **9:15 AM - 10:30 AM IST:** Opening kill zone - highest volume, institutional activity
- **2:00 PM - 3:30 PM IST:** Closing kill zone - second-highest volume

### 7. Premium & Discount Zones

Using the equilibrium (50% level) of a range:
- **Premium Zone (above 50%):** Sell zone - price is expensive
- **Discount Zone (below 50%):** Buy zone - price is cheap

## ICT Application to NSE Markets

### Differences from US Markets
1. **Trading Hours:** NSE trades 9:15 AM - 3:30 PM IST (pre-market from 9:00 AM)
2. **Circuit Limits:** NSE has upper/lower circuit limits that can prevent fills
3. **No After-Hours:** Limited after-hours activity; gaps are primarily overnight
4. **FII/DII Flows:** Institutional flows are tracked publicly - use as confluence
5. **Delivery %:** High delivery percentage on OB tests confirms institutional interest

### Best ICT Setups for NSE
1. **Bullish OB + FVG Confluence:** When a bullish order block overlaps with an unfilled FVG
2. **Liquidity Sweep + OTE:** Price sweeps a swing low, enters OTE zone, and reverses
3. **MSS + OB Entry:** Market structure shift confirmed, entry on order block retest
4. **Gap Fill + OB:** Opening gap fills into an order block level

### Timeframe Mapping
| Analysis Level | Timeframe | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Higher Timeframe | Weekly/Monthly | Identify bias (bullish/bearish) |
| Trading Timeframe | Daily | Identify setups and key levels |
| Entry Timeframe | 4H/1H | Refine entry and stop-loss |

## Risk Management with ICT

- **Stop Loss:** Always below/above the order block (with buffer)
- **Position Size:** Based on distance to stop loss, max 1-2% capital risk
- **Targets:** Use opposing order blocks or liquidity pools as targets
- **Confluence:** Only trade when 2+ ICT concepts align (OB + FVG + OTE)

---

## Limitations

- ICT concepts are **discretionary** - they require practice and context
- Not all order blocks hold; use confluence for higher-probability trades
- Works best on liquid, large-cap stocks (NIFTY 50 constituents)
- Always combine with risk management - no setup is 100% reliable
